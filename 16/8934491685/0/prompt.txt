## Problem

After <issue id="89e1f4f4-8b04-4c5b-9931-7f4f837de45f">ABA-110</issue> (SBC (Stock-Based Compensation) strip) and <issue id="e78e36cd-49f1-441f-96a9-317acc594230">ABA-111</issue> (growth-rate cap) landed, `/stock:model` now produces honest but **stark-looking** scenario outputs. Example META 2026-05-17:

* Bear IV (Intrinsic Value): $184
* Base IV: $393
* Bull IV: $778
* Current price: $614

Reading the base case in isolation ("$393 vs $614 → 36% overvalued") is misleading without the trajectory context. The base case mechanically applies the consensus growth ceiling in Y2 (META: 52% trailing → 18% capped), which looks like a cliff but is a smoothing artifact of the spec. A reader anchored on "base = realistic central case" will misread it as "the model thinks META is 36% overvalued" when the actual message is "the market is pricing META as a bull case — here's what has to keep going right."

## Why now

Thesis construction depends on **explaining how the numbers got where they got** — more than the numbers themselves. Without visuals, the reader has to do the unpacking in their head; with visuals, the trajectory and the asymmetry are immediately legible.

User quote (2026-05-17 session): *"reading a 'base case' that's so far from the current stock price has some context — it's unlikely that a company that has kept massive momentum in growth will suddenly stop, so this is important to put into context."*

## Scope

Add three visuals to the report UI for ESTABLISHED-path Model output:

1. **CAGR (Compound Annual Growth Rate) glidepath** — line chart showing trailing-3y CAGR (clean, SBC-stripped), then Y1→Y5 projected CAGR for each scenario (bear/base/bull). Annotate cap point ("capped from 52.1% by 18% fallback ceiling" for the base line).
2. **FCF (Free Cash Flow) margin trajectory** — area chart showing 3y historical reported vs clean FCF margin, with a constant-margin extension for Y1→Y5. Visually surfaces that base case assumes flat-line margins (no operating leverage) — and invites the user to consider whether they buy that assumption.
3. **Scenario fan chart** — cone/fan plot of Y1→Y5 FCF projection for bear/base/bull, with the corresponding terminal-year discounted-to-PV (Present Value) markers, and the current price line for reference.

## Acceptance criteria

1. UI renders the three visuals for any covered ticker's latest `reports/TICKER_YYYYMMDD.json` where `stages.model` is present.
2. Cap-point annotation reads from `stages.model.growth_rate` block (added in <issue id="e78e36cd-49f1-441f-96a9-317acc594230">ABA-111</issue>) — specifically `trailing_3y_cagr`, `applied_base_cagr`, `cap_source`.
3. FCF margin chart reads clean and reported series from `stages.model.fcf_margin_ttm` (clean) and `stages.model.fcf_margin_ttm_reported` (reported, added in <issue id="89e1f4f4-8b04-4c5b-9931-7f4f837de45f">ABA-110</issue>).
4. Scenario fan chart sources from `stages.model.scenarios.{bear,base,bull}` (existing).
5. Each visual has a one-sentence caption explaining the assumption it surfaces (not the chart mechanics — the *thesis* implication).
6. Pre-profit variant either gets its own analogous visuals or is explicitly scoped out (decide during design).

## Out of scope

* Glidepath shaping (introducing year-by-year CAGR taper instead of flat Y2-Y5) — separate ticket if worth doing
* Adding a margin-expansion lever to *base* case — separate methodology ticket if worth doing
* Sensitivity heatmap (WACC × g) — separate ticket

## Methodology footnote (for the reader)

The base case is intentionally a **"stop overdelivering"** anchor:

* Margins flat at today's clean FCF margin (no operating-leverage assumption)
* Growth decelerates from trailing print to consensus-5y ceiling (or 18% fallback) in Y2
* Discount rate at WACC (Weighted Average Cost of Capital) baseline

The bull case is where margin-expansion + sustained-growth assumptions live. This is by design — it forces the thesis to articulate which levers it's betting on, rather than baking optimism into the central case.
