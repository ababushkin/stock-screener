# META Playbook

**Coverage status:** ESTABLISHED · INCUMBENT  
**Thesis in one line:** Family of Apps cash machine + Reality Labs binary option + AI ad-targeting accuracy lift, with regulatory overhang as a recurring drag but not a structural break.

---

## 1. Business Architecture

### Segment mix (approximate FY2025 basis)

| Segment | Revenue share | Operating income share | Growth trajectory |
|---|---|---|---|
| Family of Apps (FoA) — advertising | ~97% | ~110%+ (absorbs RL losses) | Mid-to-high teens |
| Reality Labs (RL) | ~3% | −25% (operating loss ~$17–20B/yr) | Near-zero revenue; rising investment |

FoA advertising is the only cash-generating segment. RL is a long-duration binary bet: either smart glasses / AR / VR achieve commercial scale (2028+) or the division is wound down / spun off. The DCF base case values FoA only; RL optionality is a qualitative upside that is not modelled in the central case.

### Key revenue drivers within FoA

1. **DAP (Daily Active People)** — the volume metric. Consensus expects low-to-mid single-digit YoY growth from a ~3.4B base. Below 3% is a warning; above 5% is a positive surprise.
2. **ARPU (Average Revenue Per User)** — the monetisation metric, driven by ad price (CPMs), ad load, and AI-targeting ROAS improvements. NA/EU ARPUs are 10–20× rest-of-world; mix shift toward APAC depresses blended ARPU.
3. **AI ad-targeting** — Meta's AI-driven ad-targeting (Advantage+ suite) claims a measurable ROAS lift for advertisers. This is the swing factor in the base case: if advertisers continuously validate ROAS improvements, ad budgets shift toward Meta even at higher CPMs.

## 2. Capex-Cycle Position

Meta is in an **accelerating investment phase** (2024–2027). Data-centre and AI infrastructure capex is guided to $60–65B in 2025, up from $38B in 2024. This depresses FCF margin below steady-state.

**Normalised-margin thesis:** The `terminal_margin` override of 28% reflects a thesis-consistent steady-state SBC-stripped FCF margin once the current capex cycle digests (2027+). The TTM clean FCF margin (typically 22–25% at peak capex) understates this — hence the override prevents anchoring the DCF on a trough-margin base.

**Risk:** If AI capex is structural and perpetual (not cyclical), 28% terminal margin never materialises. The bear case's narrative captures this via compressed Y1 anchor.

## 4. Sell-Side Disagreement Axes

The real arguments the sell-side is having on META right now:

1. **Capex duration:** Is the $60B+ infrastructure spend a one-time catch-up (2024–2026) after which capex/revenue normalises ≤15%? Or is AI infrastructure a structural annual commitment that permanently compresses FCF margins? Bulls model mean-reversion; bears model a new capex regime.

2. **AI ROAS sustainability:** Is the Advantage+ ROAS lift a durable structural advantage (AI is a moat) or a cyclical tailwind that competitors (Google, TikTok) will match within 18–24 months? Bulls model permanent monetisation acceleration; bears model CPM normalisation.

3. **Regulatory endpoint:** Is the regulatory risk a repeated-fines tax (~2–3% of FCF/year) that the stock already prices in, or is structural separation (Instagram/WhatsApp dissolution) a non-trivial probability? The market's current implied probability for hard structural action appears to be <10%, which is where the disagreement sits.

4. **Reality Labs terminal value:** Is RL worth zero (a recurring cash drain that should be shut down), a negative value (capex overhang reduces equity value), a call option (worth $20–50B if spatial computing achieves scale), or a strategic platform (worth $100B+ in a 10-year horizon)? This disagreement is the largest source of cross-analyst target-price dispersion.

5. **DAP ceiling:** Does the ~3.4B DAP figure represent near-saturation in developed markets (implying ARPU must carry all growth), or is there a meaningful tail of untapped engagement in South/Southeast Asia and Africa that sustains volume growth for another 5 years?

## 5. Failure Modes

These are the thesis-breaking scenarios that the DCF does not capture because they are discrete rather than smooth:

- **DAP deceleration below 3% YoY** — the monetisation ARPU lever requires volume to remain relevant; sustained sub-3% DAP growth signals a platform that is mature without the ARPU ceiling to offset it.
- **EU structural separation** — a binding remedy requiring interoperability or data separation between Facebook/Instagram/WhatsApp would reduce the FoA bundle's CPM efficiency and potentially require structural divestitures at depressed multiples.
- **US acquisition reversal** — the most damaging tail risk; a forced unwinding of Instagram or WhatsApp would bifurcate cash flows, require separate capex programmes, and likely destroy synergy value.
- **Reality Labs structural capex** — if the $17–20B/year RL operating loss is not a cyclical investment but a permanent run-rate, steady-state FCF is systematically overstated in all scenarios.
- **AI-generated content flood** — if GenAI content degrades engagement quality (user fatigue, ad-adjacency concerns), the engagement-monetisation loop could weaken faster than the current data suggests.

## 7. Historical Reset-and-Recover Priors

| Episode | Drawdown | Catalyst | Recovery | Lesson |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 2018 Cambridge Analytica / GDPR | −43% peak-to-trough | Privacy scandal + regulatory reaction | Full recovery in ~12 months | Regulatory events reset the stock sharply but do not break the ad-monetisation flywheel if DAP holds |
| 2022 "Year of Efficiency" reset | −76% peak-to-trough (2021–2022) | Reality Labs losses disclosed + DAP YoY decline (Q1 2022, first ever) + macro ad recession | Recovery to all-time highs by 2023 | When Zuckerberg pivots to cost discipline, execution follows quickly; the reset creates a trough-margin base year that inflates trailing FCF CAGRs for 2–3 years afterward (this is the ABA-111 cap rationale for META) |
| 2023 AI-reset rerating | +194% in calendar year | "Year of Efficiency" cost cuts + AI ad-targeting ROAS inflection + broader AI rerating | — | The stock rewards operational discipline and AI-monetisation evidence faster than consensus updates |

**Mean-reversion implication:** META has consistently recovered from regulatory and operational resets within 12–24 months when the core DAP/ARPU flywheel remains intact. The recovery speed is higher when (a) management demonstrates cost discipline and (b) DAP does not sustain a multi-quarter decline. Bear scenarios that assume a permanent structural break (dissolution, permanent engagement loss) are qualitatively different from cyclical reset scenarios — the historical prior does not apply to a forced structural outcome.
