Key revenue drivers

OQ6 Data Gap — Search CPM / Click Volumes

2. AI Investment Cycle Position

Alphabet spent ~$91B in FY2025 capex, ~70% AI-attributed per public guidance. This depresses TTM clean FCF (Free Cash Flow) margin and creates a two-sided uncertainty — the normalisation path depends on which margin thesis proves correct.

▲ Path A — Base Case

Cost-temporary

AI inference cost-per-query declines on TPU hardware scaling and model-efficiency curves (distillation, quantisation). Capex/revenue mean-reverts post-2027 as the build phase completes. AI Overviews maintain or improve ad monetisation density per query. Terminal margin recovers toward 30% SBC (Stock-Based Compensation)-stripped FCF.

▼ Path B — Bear Case

Cost-permanent

AI serving costs are structurally elevated and do not decay at scale. Competitive pressure forces continued reinvestment. AI-style answering reduces click-through rates, compressing ad density and CPC simultaneously. Terminal margin stabilises in the low-20s. Basis for scenario_axes.bear.terminal_margin: 0.22.

Path A Evidence Gate
Capex/revenue ratio declining from FY2025 peak, sustained across two consecutive annual periods post-FY2026. Ratio stabilising above 18% of revenue through FY2028 = Path B signal. Gross-margin segmentation at the AI-products level (not currently disclosed) would be the most direct evidence.

4. Sell-Side Disagreement Axes

Interface Monetisation Cost Ecosystem Cloud
Interface

(i) Interface disruption

Do AI assistants become the primary discovery layer, displacing Google Search for high-intent commercial queries?

Bull
AI Overviews and AI Mode keep users inside Google surfaces for commercial-intent queries. "Where do you start a purchase query" surveys remain Google-dominant. Competitors capture curiosity and informational queries — not the ones that produce CPC revenue.
Bear
AI-native interfaces (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, in-OS assistants) capture meaningful commercial-intent query share. Users who stop clicking to Google for high-intent searches stop generating ad impressions entirely.
Signal
Structural vs. noise: Monotonic commercial-intent query-share migration sustained ≥3 quarters; "first-place-I-ask" data from third-party consumer panels specifically for purchase-research / product-comparison intent; revenue-per-search declining even in macro-stable ad-spend periods. All three together = structural. Any one alone = noise.
Monetisation

(ii) Monetisation compression

Even at stable query share, does AI-style answering structurally compress revenue-per-search?

Bull
AI Overviews maintain or improve revenue-per-search through better ad-intent matching. Users who receive AI answers and then click are higher-intent buyers. Blended RPM (Revenue Per Mille) holds or expands.
Bear
AI summaries satisfy the user's need without a click. Ad slot count per query falls as above-the-fold content becomes AI-generated. CPC compresses as advertiser competition for fewer slots declines.
Signal
Structural vs. noise: Revenue-per-search trend over ≥2 quarters filtered against macro by comparing YoY growth to peer ad platforms (Meta, Snap, Pinterest) in the same periods. A decline tracking macro peers = cycle noise. A decline diverging negatively from peers during macro-stable ad spend = compression signal. (Absolute CPM / click volumes not disclosed — OQ6 gap.)
Cost

(iii) Cost disruption

Does AI inference cost remain structurally elevated, preventing margin recovery even if revenue holds?

Bull
Inference cost-per-query declines on TPU hardware scaling and model-efficiency curves (distillation, quantisation, smaller purpose-built models). Capex/revenue mean-reverts post-2027 as the fixed-cost build phase completes. Operating leverage reasserts.
Bear
AI serving cost is a permanent elevated line item — each AI query is computationally costlier than a keyword lookup by a structurally different order of magnitude. Competitive pressure forces continued reinvestment. Cost-efficiency gains are competed away in product quality, not margin expansion.
Signal
Structural vs. noise: Capex/revenue ratio post-FY2026 across two consecutive annual periods. Declining toward ≤15% of revenue = Path A signal. Stabilising above 18% through FY2028 = Path B signal.
Ecosystem

(iv) Ecosystem and distribution displacement

Does regulatory/competitive erosion of Google's distribution advantages structurally weaken default-placement economics?

Bull
Distribution moats hold: Chrome default-search leverage continues, Apple default-search-engine payment (~$18–20B/year, market estimate) survives in modified form, Android OEM agreements sustain first-call position. Regulatory remedies stay at the margins — conduct rules, not structural separation.
Bear
DOJ remedies impair default-placement economics materially: payments-for-default restricted or banned, Chrome divested or usage-gated, Android default-app leverage limited. The structural question is whether distribution advantages durably erode — the specific remedy form matters less than the functional outcome.
Signal
Structural vs. noise: Actual remedies imposed with material appeals exhausted (not just initial rulings); third-party measurement of default-search-engine market share in the 12–24 months post-remedy implementation. Court rulings under appeal with no operational change = procedural noise. Operational changes to Chrome default or Apple TAC (Traffic Acquisition Cost) payment = structural signal.
Cloud

(v) Cloud differentiation

Is GCP developing durable economic differentiation, or participating in an AI infrastructure cycle where margins normalise?

Bull
TPU + Vertex AI + Gemini-on-Cloud create stack-level differentiation that justifies sustained pricing power. Enterprise customers building Gemini-powered workflows create switching-cost stickiness analogous to AWS-native lock-in. GCP margin expands sustainably toward AWS/Azure levels (high-20s to low-30s).
Bear
GCP participates in the AI capex cycle as a capacity provider without the enterprise-grade trust, developer ecosystem depth, or data-gravity advantages of AWS or Azure. Margins normalise to mid-cycle hyperscaler economics (mid-teens). GCP is a strategic hedge, not a durable Search offset.
Signal
Structural vs. noise: GCP operating margin trajectory across ≥4 consecutive quarters at scale. Below 15% by FY2027 = weakens Cloud-offset assumption. Above 20% by FY2027 = strengthens bull case.

5. Failure Modes

Discrete thesis-breaking scenarios the DCF does not capture as smooth-curve adjustments. Mapped to the 4-layer AI-disruption taxonomy.

Interface

Interface displacement (structural)

Multi-quarter monotonic migration of commercial-intent queries to AI-native interfaces — invalidates the base revenue growth assumption. No historical analogue: prior challengers (Bing, Yahoo relaunch) did not change user behaviour at the interface layer at scale.

Monetisation

Monetisation compression (structural)

AI Overview / AI Mode rollout compresses revenue-per-search durably; ad density falls; CPC declines in parallel. Can coexist with stable query volumes — making it harder to detect via search-share data alone.

Cost

Cost disruption (structural)

AI capex/revenue ratio stays elevated beyond FY2028 with no margin recovery. Produces joint revenue-and-margin pressure when combined with monetisation compression — captured by scenario_axes.bear compressing both growth_ceiling and terminal_margin simultaneously.

Ecosystem

Distribution remedy

DOJ remedies impair default-placement economics materially (Chrome divestiture, payments-for-default banned, AdTech forced unwind). Structural impairment of distribution economics is the failure condition — not any specific legal calendar date.

Ecosystem

Cloud commoditisation

GCP margins fail to expand durably above mid-teens by FY2027 — eliminates the second-earnings-engine optionality. If GCP remains mid-margin, the base case requires Search to sustain its full earnings contribution with no structural offset.

Ecosystem

Geo-regulatory contagion

EU DMA (Digital Markets Act) / India CCI / other-jurisdiction remedies replicate the US distribution-economics erosion pattern. The US DOJ case creates a precedent and negotiating template; a coordinated multi-jurisdiction wave could impair non-US revenue streams not currently in the bear-case model.

Why both dimensions compress in the bear case
Interface displacement causes joint compression of growth ceiling (query share migrates) and terminal margin (monetisation compresses + costs remain elevated). A symmetric −5pp terminal-margin delta off 30% (= 25%) understates this joint structural-break shape. Ad recession + iOS privacy reset
Macro ad-spend contraction + Apple ATT (App Tracking Transparency) headwinds to ad-targeting quality. Recovery on ad-market rebound and YouTube brand-advertiser return. Lesson: Cyclical ad recessions recover on macro normalisation; the underlying monetisation mechanics remained intact throughout.

2023
Bing + ChatGPT integration scare
Microsoft integrated GPT-4 into Bing; sell-side concern about Search displacement caused a multiple de-rate. Recovered as Bing share gains remained marginal and Google announced Bard/Gemini roadmap. Lesson: A credible competitive interface challenge de-rates the multiple — but did not structurally impair query economics at the monetisation layer. This episode is the closest analogue to the current AI-disruption debate; its resolution (Bing share gains marginalised) cannot be extrapolated to a scenario where AI assistants are natively integrated at the OS, browser, and search-bar level at scale.

Structural Caveat — When Prior Priors Don't Apply
These priors apply only when query-origination behaviour is structurally stable. Each prior episode recovered because mean-reverting ad economics operated on an intact query base. Interface displacement would break the recovery mechanism itself. If Search query share migrates because users adopt AI assistants as their primary discovery layer, there is no historical precedent for Google recovering from durable query-origination share loss to a competing interface. The 2023 Bing episode shows a failed interface challenge — it cannot be extrapolated to a successful one at the OS/browser/search-bar integration level.
